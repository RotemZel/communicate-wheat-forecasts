Understanding the Forecasts

Each figure below opens a detailed, interactive chart. They are organised from the broadest view down to the most specific, so you can explore at whatever level of detail suits you best.

Level 1 · Overview What drives wheat prices overall?

"Which factors matter most, across all years and time horizons?"

A bird's-eye view showing which factors most consistently push wheat prices up or down. Covers all forecast months and horizons at once. Colours indicate the strength and direction of each factor's influence.

Open chart → Level 1 · Cross-check Confirming the big picture

"Do different methods agree on which factors matter most?"

The same broad overview as above, but generated using a different analytical method. When both charts agree, you can be more confident in the result. Useful for verifying patterns seen in the overview.

Open chart → Level 2 · By month and horizon What drives prices for a specific month?

"Which factors matter for the 3-month-ahead forecast made each September, and how consistent are they across years?"

Focuses on a chosen forecast month and time horizon. Shows how each factor's influence has varied from year to year, using a distribution of historical results. You can see both the typical pattern and the exceptions.

Open chart → Level 2 · Cross-check Confirming the monthly pattern

"Does the monthly picture hold up under a different method?"

The same month-and-horizon view as above, using a second analytical approach. Comparing the two charts helps identify which findings are robust and which may be method-specific.

Open chart → Level 3 · Single factor How does one factor affect the forecast?

"How do changes in, say, production levels translate into changes in the price forecast?"

Zooms in on a single factor to show how its value relates to its influence on the forecast. Reveals whether the relationship is simple and linear, or whether it changes at certain thresholds. Useful for understanding why a specific driver matters.

Open chart → Level 4 · Specific forecast Why did the model predict this, for this month?

"Why did AGRICAF predict a 20% wheat price increase in March 2022?"

The most detailed view. Explains a single forecast for a chosen month, horizon, and year. Shows which factors pushed the prediction up or down, by how much, and how the observed values of those factors compare to historical norms. For forensic analysis of specific events.
