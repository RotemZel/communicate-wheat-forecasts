How AGRICAF Works

AGRICAF is a four-stage pipeline that transforms raw, publicly available data into explained commodity price forecasts. It begins by assembling and harmonising inputs from multiple sources — trade statistics, production figures, stock reports, and monthly price histories — into consistent predictor datasets. A screening stage then identifies which variables genuinely matter for each commodity, calendar month, and forecast horizon, using cross-validated ensemble ranking to reduce overfitting risk before any forecast is generated. The forecasting stage combines up to ten statistical and machine learning models under expanding-window cross-validation to produce monthly price predictions up to twelve months ahead, automatically selecting the most accurate model for each month-horizon pair. Finally, every forecast is explained using tailored model-agnostic interpretability methods, organised into four communication levels that range from a global annual overview to a single instance-level prediction, so that results are accessible to researchers, policymakers, and practitioners alike.
