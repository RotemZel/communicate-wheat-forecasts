How AGRICAF Works

AGRICAF is a four-stage process that turns raw, publicly available data into price forecasts with plain-language explanations. It starts by collecting and harmonising information from many sources, including trade statistics, production figures, stock reports, and monthly price histories. A screening step then identifies which of those sources actually help predict prices for each commodity, season, and time horizon, discarding the rest so the model is not misled by noise. The forecasting stage combines up to ten different statistical and machine learning methods to produce monthly price predictions up to twelve months ahead, automatically selecting the best-performing approach for each situation. Finally, every forecast is accompanied by an explanation: which factors pushed the price up or down, by how much, and how that compares to historical patterns. These explanations are presented at four levels of detail, from a broad annual overview to a forensic breakdown of a single forecast, so that the results are useful whether you are a policymaker, a researcher, or a market practitioner.

Stage 1
Gathering the Data
Prices, production, stocks, and trade statistics are collected and aligned into a consistent timeline for each commodity.
Stage 2
Selecting What Matters
Out of dozens of candidate factors, only those that genuinely improve forecast accuracy are kept. The rest are discarded.
Stage 3
Generating Forecasts
Ten models are tested on historical data. The best-performing model for each month and horizon is selected automatically.
Stage 4
Explaining the Results
Each forecast comes with a breakdown of which factors drove it, at four levels of detail from broad to specific.
